Distell | Non-Profit Sustainability Programme

Overview

Building Capacity & Resilience in the Non-Profit Sector

Africa's leading producer of award-winning alcoholic brands, Distell, recognised that there was a need to build capacity and resilience amongst the social change organisations they supported — many of whom were beginning their journey towards a social enterprise model.

Distell's CSI department took up the challenge to maximise the impact of their Non-Profit beneficiaries by investing in a Non-Profit Sustainability Programme.

They partnered with Social Enterprise Academy to offer learning programmes and mentorship in the areas of leadership, measuring social impact, and social enterprise — enabling twelve non-profits to become more financially sustainable whilst increasing their social impact.

What the Programme Delivered

🏛️
Leadership Development

Building the skills and confidence of non-profit leaders to guide their organisations towards greater impact.

📊
Measuring Social Impact

Equipping organisations with tools to measure, communicate and improve their community impact.

🌱
Social Enterprise Models

Supporting the transition towards financially sustainable social enterprise business models.

Read some of the case studies from members of the non-profit organisations that participated in the Distell Non-Profit Sustainability Programme.

The days are over when CSI departments can throw money into a black hole to tick a box. If we can help NPOs to be more sustainable they will have more impact.

— Nicole Solomons, Sustainable Socio-Economic Development at Distell
